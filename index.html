WHAT WE DO

Here, at Cortex Technologies, our main goal is to provide a comfortable, accessible, affordable and easy-to-use solution for those in need of a bionic arm. The BioArm is the perfect nexus between machine learning, brain-computer interfaces and user experience. BioArm is a bionic arm that can be controlled using one's thoughts alone.

The Real Heroes

  • Devendra

    At an early age of 8, Devendra had to get his left arm amputated as he accidentally touched a live electric cable and was electrocuted. The first Indian Paralympian to win two gold medals at the Paralympics, he won his first gold in the javelin throw at the 2004 Summer Paralympics in Athens, becoming only the second gold medalist at the Paralympics for his country. At the 2016 Summer Paralympics in Rio de Janeiro, he won a second gold medal in the same event, bettering his previous record.

    Devendra Jhajharia Indian Paralympic javelin thrower

  • Suyash

    Suyash suffering serious injuries to his upper limbs after getting accidentally electrocuted by unprotected wiring at a construction site. Both Suyash’s arms had to be amputated. Suyash, however, did not give up on his dreams and his love for swimming. Suyash became the first Indian para swimmer to record the ‘A’ qualifying mark for 2016 Paralympics in Rio and left a mark on the biggest stage of them all. Suyash also won a Silver medal at the 2017 IDM Berlin World Para Swimming World Series and represented India at the 2017 IPC World Para Swimming Championships in Mexico City.

    Suyash Sahu Indian Para swimmer
